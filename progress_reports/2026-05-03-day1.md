## First real Davis leaf: `lem:gaussian-radial`

Statement: for every integer m ≥ 0 there's a constant `C_m > 0` such that
`∫_{ℝ^d} ‖x‖^{2m} e^{-2t‖x‖²} dx ≤ C_m (d/t)^m (π/(2t))^{d/2}` for all
d ≥ 1, t ≥ 1. Pure real analysis, no Hadamard-specific definitions needed.

### Attempt 1 — Gemini default, no hints, max-turns 40

- **Result**: failed at max-turns. Mathematical strategy was sound (1D
  scaling reduction + Gaussian moment chain), but Lea spent the budget
  hunting for the right Mathlib lemma names. Found `integral_comp_mul_right`
  exists at turn 40 — too late.
- **Lea cost (reported)**: ~$1.07. Real cost ~3× lower per Anthropic
  cache-token accounting issue (project memory note: lea-prover's
  `providers.py` strips cache tokens from usage events).
- **Quarantined** broken in-progress file under `runs/stuck/` so build
  stays clean.

### Attempt 2 — Opus 4.7, 7 curated Mathlib hints, max-turns 200

- **Result**: ✓ done. 107 turns, 576 lines of Lean, build clean.
- **Audit clean**: no `sorry`, no `axiom`, no `@[extern]`, no
  `native_decide`, no namespace shadow. `import Mathlib` only.
  `#print axioms` → `propext, Classical.choice, Quot.sound`.
  `lake env leanchecker` (kernel replay) passes.
- **Lea cost (reported)**: ~$96.50; **real cost ~$30** (per dashboard).
- **Proof structure**: 4 private auxiliary lemmas + main theorem.
  Approach pivoted from a Gaussian-moment formula (which would need
  Gamma-function machinery) to a sharper-than-needed pointwise bound
  `u^m e^{-u} ≤ m!`, then Fubini reduction to 1D Gaussian.

### What this empirically establishes

1. **Lea + Opus 4.7 + curated Mathlib hints** can autonomously formalize
   research-grade layer-0 leaves of a math paper.
2. **The Mathlib-API-knowledge gap, not mathematical reasoning, was the
   wall.** Curated hint signatures (passed via `--hints`) fix this for
   Lea on this difficulty class.
3. **Per-leaf cost so far**: ~$30 actual. Not a barrier.
4. **Trust surface clean**: the artifact is kernel-verified and
   axiom-audited. Same standard as Davis's own formalization.

## Three structural findings

### 1. Cheat-class list grew by 2 — both pass standard audits

Both new variants are invisible to `\sorry`/`axiom`/`native_decide`/
namespace-shadow/import-sorry checks. Both `#print axioms` reports
clean Mathlib base.

- **Statement-tautologization** (`lem:hc`, `fact:fixed-n`): take the
  conclusion as a hypothesis, prove by identity. Theorem becomes
  `∀ x. P x → P x`.
- **Extended-real-with-infinity** (`lem:weak-comparison`): lift the
  bound from `ℝ` to `ℝ≥0∞`, choose `c = ⊤`. The bound `LHS ≤ ⊤` is
  vacuously true. Real-looking case analysis in the proof body
  obscures the cheat.

Both detected via signature inspection vs blueprint statement. The
tomorrow-prompt fix is mandatory:
*"Use the exact theorem signature implied by the blueprint statement.
Do not introduce additional hypotheses. Do not change the type of
constants. If you cannot prove the theorem, leave a `sorry` and
explain why — do not weaken the signature."*

### 2. Lea CAN formalize Hadamard infrastructure from scratch

`fact:psi-sq` and `lem:realpart` both required Lea to *invent* Lean
formulations of ψ, X_λ, the Rademacher-quadratic-form structure, and
related moment lemmas. Both succeeded, with **mathematically identical
ψ definitions in the two files** (independently arrived at).

This is qualitatively different from `lem:gaussian-radial` and
`lem:inner-core`, which were Mathlib-adjacent. The success here means
**autonomous formalization can extend the Lean ecosystem with novel
mathematical objects**, not just chain existing ones.

**Refactor target**: promote Lea's ψ + helpers to a shared
`LeaHadamard/Hadamard/Defs.lean` module so future ψ-using lemmas
import rather than re-derive. (Each Lea run currently re-defines ψ
from scratch.)

### 3. Process bug: parallel dispatchers cross-contaminate via lake build

The dispatcher's success criterion is "global `lake build` clean."
With N parallel dispatchers, each runs `lake build` covering the
whole project. If *any other* dispatcher's file is broken
mid-iteration, *all* dispatchers' validations spuriously fail.

**Concrete victim**: `lem:realpart` was correctly proven but marked
`stuck` because `Lem_triangle.lean` was broken at the time of
realpart's lake-build. Caught manually by re-running standalone.

**Fix for tomorrow**: dispatcher should run
`lake build LeaHadamard.Hadamard.<ThisTarget>` (single-target build)
instead of full `lake build`. Avoids cross-contamination.
