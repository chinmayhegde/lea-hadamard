## What this tells us about the cliff narrative

Day-1 mental model: "infrastructure cliff" between Mathlib-chaining
(cheap) and ecosystem extension (expensive ~5-10×).

Day-2 update: **There is no single cliff. There is a gradient of
content classes**, each with its own cost-per-line:

1. **Algebraic identities** (Stages 1, 2, 7a) — ~$0.02-0.03/line.
2. **Real-analytic inequalities with `nlinarith`** (Stage 4) — ~$0.01/line.
3. **Polynomial-structure induction** (Stage 5) — ~$0.04/line.
4. **Measure-theoretic moment proofs** (Stage 7b) — ~$0.04/line.
5. **General-degree orthogonality with involutions** (Stage 8a) — ~$0.01/line.
6. **Combinatorial enumeration** (ordered-cycle ahead) — projected
   ~$0.05-0.10/line based on layer-0 `lem:triangle` precedent.

All stages today landed at <$0.05/line, comparable to or cheaper than
the cumulative-layer-0 average (~$0.06/line). **The "cliff" framing was
wrong about cost across all infra classes we tested.** What's left
ahead is increasingly Davis-specific paper-content rather than
reusable Mathlib material — and that's where the next genuinely-hard
question sits.
